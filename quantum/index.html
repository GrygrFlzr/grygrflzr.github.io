Quantum Computing

What it is and why it's important

Powered by Qubits

A qubit is a unit of quantum information. While a bit can only be in one state or the other, qubits can be in a superposition of both at the same time!

Not a Replacement

Quantum computers only have a speed advantage over classical computers when doing specific types of calculations.

Fragile

Qubits will return a classical value upon being observed. It must not be disturbed by any outside force or the whole system will break down.

Forget everything you knew about physics.

At the quantum level, the usual laws of physics don't apply.

Quantum Physics is counter-intuitive; it has properties of both particles and waves, and what affects one particle can change another. This phenomenon is called Quantum Entanglement.

Quantum Entanglement is the connection between 2 particles in superposition. When one particle is observed to be in a 'spin-up' position, the other particle will be in a 'spin-down' position. This effect is instantaneous — faster than the speed of light — no matter the distance, but does not enable faster-than-light transmission of classical data.

If quantum mechanics hasn't profoundly shocked you, you haven't understood it yet.

The D-Wave Quantum Processor is operated at 20 millikelvin. These low temperatures slow down particles enough to allow quantum mechanics to take place.

Breaking the RSA Algorithm.

The world standard for encryption relies on prime factors.

It is trivial to calculate the product of two prime numbers with a conventional computer. Finding the prime factors of a composite number (in this case a semi-prime) takes exponential time — which means it can be used in public key encryption. This is one of the few algorithms where quantum computers can execute parallel calculations simultaneously to break the security of the RSA algorithm.

The National Security Agency of the USA has expressed interest in breaking encryption standards for "national security" reasons, and quantum computing is certainly an option they will not overlook.
