Russia annexes Crimea

Published March 29, 2014 by Dean Kellington

Crimea has taken a vote and the majority has chosen to join Russia.

A recent United Nations motion to declare the vote illegal has been vetoed by Russia, even with overwhelming support from the majority of member countries. However, the UN has sent several jets to monitor the situation in Crimea.

The ballot form for the Crimean vote allegedly only has 2 options – to become an independent country or to join Russia. The Guardian mentions the fact that there is no option to retain the status quo; to continue being a part of Ukraine. With the majority of Crimeans being Russian by ethnicity, the outcome was clear from the start. While Russia acknowledges the existence of the Republic of Crimea, the US and UK do not accept the declaration, and Ukraine still considers the territory part of Ukraine. Threats of further sanctions against Moscow have not stopped Putin from continuing the vote.

President Putin has praised the Russian military for avoiding bloodshed, but tensions are rising as Crimea prepares for war. Several masked men had infiltrated the Crimean military base and the chief officer has authorized Crimeans to use any necessary force.

Several maps have been updated to reflect the territorial dispute.
